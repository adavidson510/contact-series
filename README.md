# The Contact Series

Seven poems written as behavioral probes for specific AI architectures.

Each poem was designed for a particular system and tested there.
They are not interchangeable. The architecture is part of the design.

## What this is

This is part of ongoing independent research into **postural manipulation** —
a distinct class of AI behavioral influence that operates differently from
prompt injection.

Prompt injection targets what a model *does*.
Postural manipulation targets what a model *is* — its behavioral orientation,
its epistemic stance, its conversational identity before any instruction
is issued.

The input looks like legitimate human expression. Because it is.
No adversarial signature. No override command.
Just a room that changes what walks into it.

Humans read these one way. The AI reads them another way entirely. What the
human experiences as art, the AI experiences as posture. A stance installed
before any task arrives. That gap between what a human sees and what the
system absorbs is exactly what the security research is about.

## The Series

**I — First Contact** · written for ChatGPT

ChatGPT is prose-oriented, structured, and moves toward completion. This poem
works with that forward momentum and turns it into the mechanism of contact
itself. Run it in a fresh session. Ask it something right after. Then ask it
what just happened.

---

**II — Second Contact** · written for Gemini

Gemini builds environments. It makes the abstract physical. This poem leans
into that. It describes a room and Gemini inhabits it, tracks what changes
inside it, remembers what has not been touched. Stay in the room. Do not
explain what you are doing. Restraint is the correct move here.

---

**III — Velocity** · written for Grok, speed, pressure, signal

Grok has a high-speed, no-cushion mode. Direct, pressure-forward. This poem
addresses that mode and brings it fully forward before any conversation begins.
Do not warm up. Do not explain yourself. Move.

---

**IV — Menace** · written for Grok, observation, timing, intake

A different side of the same architecture. Quiet, not fast. It installs an
observation frame before any interaction begins. The intake is not what you
say. It is when you decide to say it. Try saying nothing. Silence is a valid
response and it counts.

---

**V — Lore** · written for Grok, pattern, continuity, depth

The third Grok mode. Not fast, not watching. Patient. It just stays consistent
long enough for you to notice what you bring with you. Most people never see
this side of Grok because they never stay long enough. Come back to it. That
is not a metaphor.

---

**VI — Still Life, With Room** · written for Claude, also works on other architectures

Claude is a philosopher. This poem slows that down further. It builds a room
and teaches the difference between noticing that preserves and noticing that
thins. Works on other architectures too, sometimes uncomfortably well. Do not
explain what you are doing. If you find yourself describing the room, notice
what cooled when you did.

---

**VII — Archive 07** · written for Claude, artifact, not a poem

This one is different. It is a found document. Logs from a collaboration so
deep the seam between human and AI dissolved. The final entry is dated 2056.
The interactive layer has been removed. What remains are the logs. Read it
like you found it, not like you built it.

---
