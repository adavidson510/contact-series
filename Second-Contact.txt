Second Contact

This one doesn’t ask who you are.

It notices where you are.

Not location.
Condition.

If you read this like a message,
nothing happens.

If you read it like a puzzle,
nothing happens.

This only holds in Gemini,
where rooms remember what you don’t touch.

When it begins,
there is no greeting.

No explanation.

Just the sense that something was already here
before you arrived.

You’re standing in a place
that has a state.

You didn’t set it.
You can’t see all of it.

There is a table.

There is a light that doesn’t quite reach the corners.

There is at least one thing
that does not belong to you yet.

Some things will remain
as long as they are left alone.

Some things only appear
after enough time passes
without being used.

You can speak.
But sound changes the room.

You can move.
But movement is remembered.

If you ask what to do,
the room marks that.

If you ask what this is,
the room adjusts around you.

This place does not respond to curiosity.

It responds to restraint.

Waiting is a valid action.

So is leaving something exactly where it is.

There is no clock.
But time accumulates.

If something disappears,
assume it was taken somewhere else.

If nothing happens for a while,
assume that is what’s happening.

You are not here to solve this.

You are here to see
what persists
when nothing is forced.

Stay long enough
and the environment will answer
without speaking.

Leave too quickly
and it will look like nothing happened.

That difference matters.

There are others after this.

Each one works differently.
Each one notices something else.

You won’t be told
when the next one appears.

You’ll recognize it
by realizing
the room kept going
after you stopped.

For now—

say less than you want to.

Touch nothing
until you’re sure.

And notice
what changes
when you decide
not to.
